The Network

SFOF does not operate in a vacuum. It is the central node in a sophisticated and well-funded network of conservative think tanks, corporate lobbyists, and legal organizations working in concert to achieve a unified political agenda.

The Heritage Foundation

The Policy Architect

The brain trust of the anti-ESG movement, providing the intellectual and legal framework that underpins the campaign by drafting detailed model legislation for state-level allies.

Key Contributions:

  • "State Pension Fiduciary Duty Act": Redefines fiduciary duty to require "pecuniary factors only," effectively outlawing ESG goals.
  • "Eliminate Economic Boycotts Act": Punishes companies for "boycotting" politically favored industries like fossil fuels and firearms.

ALEC

The Legislative Engine

Functions as the primary distribution channel for the network's agenda. Through "pay-to-play" meetings, corporate lobbyists and state legislators collaborate to adapt and vote on model bills.

Key Contributions:

  • "Energy Discrimination Elimination Act": The template for Texas's landmark SB 13, prohibiting state contracts with firms that limit business with the fossil fuel industry.
  • Direct Leadership Overlap: ALEC's CEO, Lisa B. Nelson, also serves on the SFOF board, ensuring seamless strategic coordination.

State Attorneys General

The Legal Enforcers

A coordinated coalition of Republican state attorneys general provides the legal muscle. This "lawfare" campaign uses the power of the state to launch multi-state lawsuits and investigations.

Key Actions:

  • Antitrust Lawsuits: Framing corporate collaboration on climate change (e.g., Climate Action 100+) as an illegal "climate cartel."
  • Targeting Proxy Advisors: Launching investigations into firms like ISS and Glass Lewis, alleging they mislead clients.

Corporate Partners

A Contradiction in Terms

The network is sustained by conservative foundations and corporate sponsors. The actions of SFOF members reveal that the "pecuniary factors only" rule is a political weapon deployed selectively.

The Israel Bonds Connection:

The Development Corporation for Israel, underwriter for Israel Bonds, is a documented SFOF sponsor. SFOF treasurers in states like Florida, Ohio, and Texas have directed hundreds of millions in public funds into these bonds, justifying the investments with explicitly political language—to "show our support"—directly contradicting the legal standard they impose on others.
