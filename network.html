The Network

SFOF does not operate in a vacuum. It is the central node in a sophisticated and well-funded network of conservative think tanks, corporate lobbyists, and legal organizations working in concert to achieve a unified political agenda.

The Heritage Foundation

The Policy Architect

The Heritage Foundation serves as the brain trust of the anti-ESG movement, providing the intellectual and legal framework that underpins the entire campaign. It drafts comprehensive, detailed model legislation that is then distributed to the network's state-level allies.

Key Contributions:

  • "State Pension Fiduciary Duty Act": A model bill that legally redefines fiduciary duty to require fiduciaries to consider "only pecuniary factors," effectively outlawing the consideration of ESG goals. This model was directly implemented in states like Kentucky and South Carolina.
  • "Eliminate Economic Boycotts Act": A comprehensive template designed to punish companies for "boycotting" politically favored industries like fossil fuels, firearms, and agriculture. This model was adopted nearly verbatim by Alabama.

ALEC

The Legislative Engine

The American Legislative Exchange Council (ALEC) functions as the primary distribution channel for the network's legislative agenda. Through its "pay-to-play" meetings, corporate lobbyists and state legislators collaborate to adapt and vote on model bills, creating a highly efficient pipeline to introduce anti-ESG legislation across the country.

Key Contributions:

  • "Energy Discrimination Elimination Act": A foundational model bill designed to prohibit state contracts with financial firms that limit business with the fossil fuel industry. This became the template for Texas's landmark SB 13.
  • Direct Leadership Overlap: ALEC's CEO, Lisa B. Nelson, also serves as an At-Large member of the SFOF board, ensuring seamless strategic coordination between the legislative and executive-branch arms of the movement.

State Attorneys General

The Legal Enforcers

A coordinated coalition of Republican state attorneys general provides the legal muscle for the network. This "lawfare" campaign uses the power of the state to launch multi-state lawsuits and investigations, creating a hostile legal environment for firms that engage in ESG.

Key Actions:

  • Antitrust Lawsuits: Filing lawsuits that frame corporate collaboration on climate change (e.g., Climate Action 100+) as an illegal "climate cartel" that violates antitrust laws.
  • Targeting Proxy Advisors: Launching investigations into firms like ISS and Glass Lewis, alleging they mislead clients by promoting a "leftist worldview" under the guise of impartial advice.
  • Federal Pushback: Challenging federal rules from the SEC and Department of Labor that allow or mandate ESG considerations.

Corporate & Financial Partners
