The SFOF Nexus: Inside the Coordinated Corporate and Political War on ESG

Executive Summary

The State Financial Officers Foundation (SFOF) has evolved from a niche free-market policy group into the central command and control hub for the state-level political war against Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) investing. Founded in 2012, SFOF now serves as the indispensable coordinating body for a network of Republican state treasurers, auditors, and financial officers, equipping them with the tools, funding, and strategy to leverage the power of public office for a national ideological campaign. This report details how SFOF, through a sophisticated dual-structure non-profit model, channels millions of dollars from a cloistered network of conservative "dark money" foundations and corporate sponsors to its members. These funds support closed-door strategy sessions where model legislation is disseminated, media campaigns are planned, and a unified political message is forged.

The foundation's partnerships with powerful conservative organizations like the American Legislative Exchange Council (ALEC), The Heritage Foundation, and Consumers' Research reveal a highly organized division of labor designed to efficiently move an ideological agenda from a think tank memo to enacted state law. While SFOF presents its mission as the protection of fiduciary duty and the promotion of free markets, this investigation finds its impact to be the opposite. The anti-ESG laws championed by SFOF and its members have inflicted tangible financial harm on the very taxpayers they serve, costing states hundreds of millions of dollars in increased borrowing costs and lost economic activity. The evidence demonstrates that SFOF is not a mere educational non-profit, but a highly effective political entity designed to leverage public office to advance a specific ideological and corporate agenda, regardless of the fiscal consequences.

Section I: The Architect and the Blueprint - Organizational Structure and Leadership

From Free Markets to Culture Wars: SFOF's Origins and Strategic Pivot

SFOF was co-founded in 2012 by eight state treasurers and Derek Kreifels, then the assistant state treasurer of Kansas, with an initial contribution of a single $10,000 corporate gift. Registered as a 501(c)(3) public charity, its stated mission is to "promote fiscally responsible public policy" and "educate the public about the role of finance in a free market economy," a broad and seemingly nonpartisan mandate.

Profile of an Operator: Derek Kreifels, Co-Founder and Strategist

The architect of SFOF's structure and strategy is its co-founder and long-time CEO, Derek Kreifels. His background at the intersection of state finance and partisan politics—serving as Assistant State Treasurer of Kansas from 2010 to 2016 while also holding the position of Secretary of the Kansas Republican Party—provided the ideal experience to build such an organization.

The Dual Structure: Education and Attack (SFOF and SFOF Action)

SFOF employs a classic "dark money" organizational design to maximize its political effectiveness while protecting its funding streams. The primary entity, State Financial Officers Foundation, is a 501(c)(3) public charity, which allows it to receive tax-deductible contributions from foundations and corporations for "educational" purposes. To conduct the direct political and lobbying work forbidden for a 501(c)(3), SFOF established a sister organization, SFOF Action, in 2024. As a 501(c)(4) "social welfare" organization, SFOF Action can engage in unlimited lobbying and direct political advocacy without having to disclose its donors.

Section II: Fueling the Machine - The Financial Network

The Ideological Investors: Foundation Funding

As a 501(c)(3), SFOF is not required to disclose its donors. However, IRS filings from the grant-making foundations themselves illuminate the primary sources of its ideological backing.

Corporate Allies and Strategic Interests

A significant portion of SFOF's revenue comes from its tiered corporate sponsorship program, which grants paying corporations direct, private access to the powerful state officials who regulate their industries and manage trillions of dollars in public assets.

Section III: The Command Center - SFOF as a Coordinating Hub

The Strategy Sessions: Meetings and Conferences

The core of SFOF's coordinating activity is its semi-annual national meetings. Far from typical professional conferences, these are closed-door strategy sessions where the anti-ESG campaign is planned and refined. Attendee lists from these events reveal a potent mix of participants: SFOF's 37 member officials from 28 states, their senior staff, lobbyists and executives from corporate sponsors like J.P. Morgan and Wells Fargo, and key operatives from allied organizations such as ALEC, The Heritage Foundation, Consumers' Research, and the public relations firm CRC Advisors.

The Arsenal: Resources and Support for State Officials

SFOF provides its members with a turnkey arsenal of political tools, effectively allowing under-resourced state offices to outsource their policy and communications functions to a well-funded, private organization.

Section IV: A Web of Alliances - The Anti-ESG Ecosystem

SFOF does not operate in a vacuum. It is a critical node within a tightly integrated ecosystem of conservative organizations, each playing a specialized role. This network functions as a sophisticated policy influence supply chain, designed to move an idea from a think tank memo to an enacted state law with maximum efficiency.

Section V: The Bottom Line - Impact and Consequences

Rewriting the Rules: Influence on Public Policy

SFOF and its allies have achieved remarkable success in translating their agenda into state law. Since 2020, more than 480 anti-ESG bills have been introduced in 40 states, with dozens becoming law. In 2023 alone, 165 such bills were introduced across 37 states.

Market Disruption and Taxpayer Costs

The anti-ESG laws championed by SFOF directly contradict its stated mission of promoting "fiscally responsible public policy." Independent analyses have consistently found that these laws inflict significant financial harm on the states that enact them.

Conclusion: The Central Node of a Political Machine

The State Financial Officers Foundation is far more than an educational forum for public officials. The evidence demonstrates that it is the indispensable central node in a sophisticated political machine designed to translate the ideological goals of conservative foundations and the financial interests of specific industries into public policy.
