Uncovering State Capture

An open-source dossier mapping the State Financial Officers Foundation (SFOF). We track their funders, legislative influence, and network operations to expose state capture campaigns.

Explore the Influence Atlas

1. The Foundation

A Dual-Entity Strategy

SFOF operates as a tax-exempt 501(c)(3) foundation for fundraising and a 501(c)(4) "social welfare" organization for direct lobbying and political action. This structure maximizes its financial intake and political influence while navigating U.S. tax law.

2. The Network

An Alliance of Influence

Deeply integrated with conservative powerhouses like the American Legislative Exchange Council (ALEC) and The Heritage Foundation, SFOF serves as the "finance ministry" of a coordinated movement, weaponizing state financial offices to advance a unified agenda.

3. The Playbook

Inside-Outside Strategy

SFOF pressures corporations with national sign-on letters (the "outside game") while simultaneously promoting ALEC-drafted model legislation within statehouses (the "inside game"), creating a pincer movement to shape policy and corporate behavior.

4. The Frontlines

State-Level Operations

With affiliated officials in over half of U.S. states, SFOF's influence is widespread. From pushing anti-ESG legislation in Texas to coordinating divestment threats in West Virginia, their national agenda is executed through local power.
